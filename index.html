M. Umar Shahbaz

Technology has undoubtedly been the most influential thing in my life, but it wasn't the only one. Growing up, most kids my age only had one hobby: playing video games on their parent's phones. I, however, wasn't interested in them. I had, of course, played them but nothing about them stuck to me. Even now, if someone were to ask me “What hobbies do you have other than Robotics?”, I would say that they were Football and Reading.

It's unusual for a tech geek to be athletic, but during those days I played Football quite a lot, especially during my O levels. On the other hand, I was a frequent reader. Having read all the Harry Potter books, I had fancied novels. I was particularly interested in the genres of Fiction and Crime, with my favorite among them being the Nervernight Chronicles by Jay Kristoff.

When I started out, I couldn't possibly have known how each decision and each action would shape me into who I have become. Now I can boast a wide variety of skills and a certain amount of mastery in many fields including, but not limited to, Physics; Computer Engineering; Electrical Engineering; Mechanics as well as others. I have reached a point in my life that I would never have expected to when I chose Technology. Still, there is a burning question within me. How much further?
